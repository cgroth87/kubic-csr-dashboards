Issue Timeline & Root Causes

January 2026
Capacity Crisis — WA Rail Disruption
15 incidents across 13 loads. WA rail services went down in mid-January, causing an influx of WA-bound loads onto the road network. This consumed Kubic's available B-Double fleet, directly impacting NSW>SA capacity. 7 missed collections, 3 late arrivals, 1 equipment issue.
February 2026
Equipment Quality & Load Restraint Focus
12 incidents across 11 loads (20% improvement from Jan). Primary issues shifted to equipment quality: missing/damaged gates, blown-out curtains, insufficient load restraint, and non-rated trailers. 5 equipment incidents, 3 late with notification. A mezzanine trailer was incorrectly sent for a rear-loading job.
Early–Mid March 2026
Bhullar Linehaul Onboarded — Subcontractor Issues Spike
Bhullar Linehaul was brought on to supplement NSW>SA capacity. Unfortunately, they generated 15 incidents across inductions, equipment, late arrivals and missed loads. Bhullar accounted for 5 of 6 incidents in W11, 7 of 12 in W12, 2 of 6 in W13 and 1 of 2 in W14. Without Bhullar, core Kubic operations were W11: 1, W12: 5, W13: 4, W14: 1 — a stable and manageable level.
Late March 2026 — Action Taken
Bhullar Removed & Core Kubic Stabilising
Bhullar Linehaul has been permanently removed from the Kubic carrier network. Adjusted core Kubic trend shows W13 at just 4 incidents — a 50% reduction from the W04 peak (8). Adhoc bookings and mitigating circumstances further reduce the true issue count. The broader carrier market also struggled on this lane in March — Challenger (4), Hume (2), Bulk (2), Visy (1) all logged issues.

Root Cause Breakdown

Bhullar Linehaul (15 incidents) Subcontractor brought on for capacity but caused 50% of March NSW>SA issues. Uninducted drivers, poor equipment, missed loads. Now permanently removed from network.
Capacity & Missed (19 total Q1) WA rail disruption in Jan depleted available fleet. Subcontractor pullouts at short notice. Multiple same-day cancellations.
Equipment Issues (8 total Q1) Missing trailer gates, non-rated curtains, insufficient straps. Fleet quality being addressed with maintenance program.
Late Arrivals (16 total Q1) Delays at prior locations (Coles DC, Badgery's Creek). Driver logbook errors. 6 of 16 communicated in advance. 1 arrived within 30 min of slot.
Driver Inductions (4 excl. Bhullar) Uninducted drivers at Bradford. Pre-dispatch induction verification now implemented.
Adhoc Bookings (7 incidents) Loads booked within 24 hours notice. Higher risk of issues due to compressed planning time. Not reflective of standard contracted performance.

Actions & Talking Points

Meeting Talking Points

1. Network Strength: 7 out of 8 contracted lanes are performing to standard with minimal or zero incidents. Kubic's network reliability across VIC, QLD, and return SA corridors is strong.
2. Ingleburn > Mawson Lakes Context: January's WA rail disruption created a cascading capacity issue that disproportionately impacted the NSW>SA corridor. This was an external, industry-wide event.
3. Bhullar Linehaul Removed: A subcontractor (Bhullar Linehaul) was brought on to assist with capacity but caused 50% of all March NSW>SA issues (15 of 30). Bhullar has been permanently removed from the Kubic network. Adjusted core Kubic March NSW>SA: 15 incidents — in line with January (15) and February (12).
4. Positive Trend Direction: Adjusted weekly trend (excl. Bhullar) shows core Kubic improving: W11: 1, W12: 5, W13: 4. This compares to the W04 peak of 8. Communication has improved with more advance ETA notifications.
5. Industry Comparison: When alternate carriers covered NSW>SA loads in March, they also experienced issues (Challenger: 4, Hume: 2, Bulk: 2). CSR's own log shows 50 non-Kubic incidents in March. The log only began tracking non-Kubic carriers from March — the true Q1 figure is likely significantly higher.
6. Recovery Commitment: 58% same-day recovery rate demonstrates Kubic's commitment to finding solutions when issues arise, rather than simply leaving loads uncovered.
7. Corrective Actions Underway: Bhullar removed from network. Fleet maintenance program tightened. Pre-dispatch induction checks implemented. Improved communication protocols. Dedicated capacity allocation for the NSW>SA corridor going forward.

Australian Road Freight Industry Context (2025–2026)

Independent industry data supporting that operational challenges are sector-wide, not Kubic-specific.

Driver Shortage Crisis 28,000 unfilled truck driving positions in Australia (2024). Projected to reach 78,000 by 2029. 47% of current drivers are over 55; only 5% under 25. One in five drivers under 35 report severe psychological stress.
Source: NatRoad “The Driver Shortage Crisis in Australia”
Carrier Financial Stress 8.46% of road transport businesses exited in 12 months to Nov 2025 — 40% higher than prior year. One in 12 trucking operators closed. Elevated cost bases and razor-thin profit margins creating insolvency pressures throughout 2026.
Source: CreditorWatch / AICM Report
Rail Disruptions & Infrastructure East-West rail corridor closures for maintenance (Sep 2025). Mount Isa rail line multiple closures from flooding and derailment (Dec 2025). Cyclone Narelle disrupted Port Hedland-Geraldton corridor. These events push freight onto road, straining B-Double capacity.
Source: ARTC / Infrastructure Australia
Rising Operating Costs Diesel prices up 30–50 cents per litre. Labour shortages driving up wages. Per-mile costs increasing faster than inflation. Soft freight demand from retail and manufacturing squeezing margins further.
Source: ATA / NatRoad Pre-Budget Submission 2026–27
NatRoad “Road to 2028” Reform Plan Key issues identified: critical workforce shortages, unsustainable cost structures, aging infrastructure, maintenance backlogs at key terminals. Market expected to grow from $43.2B to $45B but structural challenges remain.
Source: NatRoad Road to 2028 Reform Plan
Visy Logistics Q1 2025 Market Update Significant operational challenges including port congestion, carrier blank sailings during Lunar New Year, and capacity reductions of 26,000 TEU on Asia-Oceania routes. Alliance changes causing missed sailings and port delays.
Source: Visy Global Logistics Q1 2025 Market Update

Prepared Responses to Potential Pushback

“Kubic has too many issues on NSW>SA” 50% of March NSW>SA issues were caused by one subcontractor (Bhullar Linehaul) who has been permanently removed. Core Kubic operations had 14 incidents in March on this lane — and the adjusted weekly trend shows clear improvement (W13: 4 incidents). When alternate carriers (Challenger, Bulk, Hume) covered our loads, they experienced 9 issues of their own on the same lane.
“Why aren’t other carriers having these problems?” They are. CSR’s own log shows 50 non-Kubic incidents in March alone, including Visy unable to cover contracted lanes, Challenger with no availability, Bulk damaging site infrastructure, and Linfox sending 4 uninducted drivers in one day. The log only started tracking non-Kubic carriers in March — the real number for Q1 is likely far higher.
“The March numbers are getting worse, not better” Three factors inflate the March numbers: (1) Bhullar Linehaul = 14 incidents (now removed), (2) expanded logging now captures all carriers and adhoc bookings, (3) several entries have mitigating context (recovered loads, arrived within 30 min, not a Kubic FO). Adjusted core Kubic NSW>SA trend: Jan 15, Feb 12, Mar 14 — stable and improving.
“The industry excuses don’t apply to your performance” 1 in 12 Australian trucking operators closed in the past 12 months. There are 28,000 unfilled driver positions nationally. Rail disruptions pushed WA freight onto road, consuming B-Double capacity. Kubic is still here, still servicing 7 of 8 lanes without issue, and investing in corrective actions for the one lane that needs attention.
